An Efficient Trajectory Replanning Framework for
Robotic Arm Non-Prehensile Transportation

Code

Abstract

Robotic non-prehensile transportation in complex environments shows significant promise for the service industry. These tasks require robotic arms to be reactive, safe, and flexible enough to navigate dynamic and complex environments while maintaining stable transportation. However, existing limited-horizon motion control methods are often ineffective at navigating in such environments. This letter introduces a framework for efficiently replanning the trajectory of robotic arms to safely and flexibly transport objects. First, a sampling-based path planner with pose constraints is designed to generate a high-quality initial trajectory that is parameterized by piecewise polynomials. Next, an optimizer refines the trajectory to improve feasibility while striving to minimize control effort and time. Notably, the original dynamics-based optimization problem is decomposed into two simpler, kinematics-based subproblems to simplify and speed up computation. Additionally, a collision detector continuously monitors the current trajectory to determine whether to replan or perform a safety stop. The detector also triggers a hardware-level emergency stop when necessary. Finally, the effectiveness and robustness of this framework are validated through extensive simulations and real-world experiments.
